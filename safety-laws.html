Global Safety Organizations

📌 Why These Organizations Matter

These international bodies define the rules that protect millions of workers worldwide. Understanding each organization's mandate helps safety professionals apply the right standard in the right context — from oil rigs in Saudi Arabia to construction sites in Bangladesh.

OSHA
Occupational Safety and Health Administration
US federal agency enforcing workplace safety laws since 1970. Sets standards, conducts inspections, and issues citations.
osha.gov →
IOSH
Institution of Occupational Safety & Health
World's largest membership body for HSE professionals, based in UK. Provides qualifications, guidance, and advocacy.
iosh.com →
NEBOSH
National Examination Board in Occupational Safety and Health
Global provider of safety qualifications. NEBOSH certificates and diplomas are recognized in over 130 countries.
nebosh.org.uk →
ISO
International Organization for Standardization
Publishes ISO 45001 — the world's leading standard for occupational health and safety management systems.
iso.org →
ILO
International Labour Organization
UN agency setting international labour standards. Its OSH conventions have been ratified by over 180 countries.
ilo.org →
ARAMCO
Saudi Aramco — World's Largest Oil & Gas Company
Operates one of the most rigorous HSE systems globally. SAEP and GI standards are benchmarks for the oil industry.
aramco.com →
SAIPEM
SAIPEM S.p.A. — Global Engineering & Construction
Italian multinational applying stringent HSSE management on offshore and onshore megaprojects worldwide.
saipem.com →
HSE
Health and Safety Executive (UK)
UK national regulator for workplace health, safety and welfare. Enforces HASAWA 1974 and related legislation.
hse.gov.uk →
