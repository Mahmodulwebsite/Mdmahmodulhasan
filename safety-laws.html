Global Safety Organizations

📌 Why These Organizations Matter

These international bodies define the rules that protect millions of workers worldwide. Understanding each organization's mandate helps safety professionals apply the right standard in the right context — from oil rigs in Saudi Arabia to construction sites in Bangladesh.

OSHA
Occupational Safety and Health Administration
US federal agency enforcing workplace safety laws since 1970. Sets standards, conducts inspections, and issues citations.
osha.gov →
IOSH
Institution of Occupational Safety & Health
World's largest membership body for HSE professionals, based in UK. Provides qualifications, guidance, and advocacy.
iosh.com →
NEBOSH
National Examination Board in Occupational Safety and Health
Global provider of safety qualifications. NEBOSH certificates and diplomas are recognized in over 130 countries.
nebosh.org.uk →
ISO
International Organization for Standardization
Publishes ISO 45001 — the world's leading standard for occupational health and safety management systems.
iso.org →
ILO
International Labour Organization
UN agency setting international labour standards. Its OSH conventions have been ratified by over 180 countries.
ilo.org →
ARAMCO
Saudi Aramco — World's Largest Oil & Gas Company
Operates one of the most rigorous HSE systems globally. SAEP and GI standards are benchmarks for the oil industry.
aramco.com →
SAIPEM
SAIPEM S.p.A. — Global Engineering & Construction
Italian multinational applying stringent HSSE management on offshore and onshore megaprojects worldwide.
saipem.com →
HSE
Health and Safety Executive (UK)
UK national regulator for workplace health, safety and welfare. Enforces HASAWA 1974 and related legislation.
hse.gov.uk →

Universal Standards
General Occupational Safety
⚠️
Fundamental Safety Principles
Universal rules applied by all major organizations
▼
  • 01Hazard assessments (JSA/JHA) must be conducted before starting any job — required by OSHA 29 CFR 1910.132 & ISO 45001. OSHA · ISO
  • 02Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) must be job-specific, inspected daily, and replaced when damaged. OSHA 1910.132
  • 03Daily Toolbox Talks (TBT) are mandatory before work begins — a core requirement in Aramco SAEP-304 and SAIPEM HSE plans. Aramco · SAIPEM
  • 04Every worker must report near misses. Silent hazards are the leading cause of future accidents — ILO Convention 155 mandates this. ILO C155
  • 05Work permits (PTW) are required for all non-routine, high-risk tasks. Verbal authorization is never sufficient. Aramco GI-6.012
  • 06Safety signage must comply with ISO 7010 color-coding standards and be visible, clean, and unobstructed. ISO 7010
  • 07Regular safety observations (STOP, BBS) must be conducted and recorded — part of IOSH and NEBOSH frameworks. IOSH · NEBOSH
  • 08Proper induction training is mandatory before site entry. No untrained worker shall begin work unsupervised. IOSH · HSE
  • 09Incident investigations must follow the ICAM or TRIPOD model to identify root causes — not blame individuals. NEBOSH · SAIPEM
  • 10Safety management systems must be reviewed at minimum annually against ISO 45001 clause 9.3 objectives. ISO 45001

🇺🇸 United States
OSHA Standards & Regulations
🏭
OSHA General Industry (29 CFR 1910)
Standards for manufacturing, warehouses, offices and general workplaces
▼
  • 01Employers must provide workplaces free from recognized serious hazards — the "General Duty Clause" Section 5(a)(1). 29 USC 654
  • 02Lockout/Tagout (LOTO): All energy sources must be isolated before maintenance — 29 CFR 1910.147. Failure leads to citations up to $15,625. 1910.147
  • 03Hazard Communication Standard (HazCom): Chemicals must be labeled with GHS pictograms. SDS must be accessible to all workers. 1910.1200
  • 04Confined Space: Atmospheric testing required before entry. An attendant must be present at all times. 1910.146
  • 05Respiratory Protection: Medical evaluation required before using respirators. Written program mandatory. 1910.134
  • 06Electrical safety: Only qualified persons may work on live circuits. Electrical panels must be clear and labeled. 1910.333
  • 07Recordkeeping: Work-related injuries must be logged in OSHA 300 Log. Fatalities reported within 8 hours. 1904
  • 08OSHA whistleblower protections: Employers cannot retaliate against workers for reporting hazards — Section 11(c). Section 11(c)
  • 09OSHA 30-Hour Training: Supervisors and safety officers in high-risk industries must complete OSHA 30H outreach. OSHA 30H
  • 10Walking/Working Surfaces: Floors must support loads, be kept clean, and have adequate drainage per 29 CFR 1910.22. 1910.22
🏗
OSHA Construction (29 CFR 1926)
Rules specific to construction sites and project-based work
▼
  • 01Fall protection required at 6 feet in construction — guardrails, safety nets, or personal fall arrest systems. 1926.502
  • 02Scaffolding must be inspected by a competent person before each shift. Load capacity tags must be posted. 1926.451
  • 03Excavations deeper than 5 feet require protective systems (shoring, sloping, or shielding). 1926.652
  • 04Steel erection: Workers must be protected from falls during steel beam placement — decking and nets required. 1926.760
  • 05Hot work (welding, cutting): Fire watch required for 30 minutes after hot work completion. Area must be cleared of flammables. 1926.352

🇬🇧 United Kingdom
IOSH — Managing Safely Framework
📗
IOSH Managing Safely Principles
For managers and supervisors responsible for people and processes
▼
  • 01Managers must understand their legal duties — UK HASAWA 1974 places responsibilities on individuals, not just organizations. HASAWA 1974
  • 02Risk assessments must be "suitable and sufficient" — documented and reviewed when conditions change. Management of Health & Safety at Work Regulations 1999. MHSWR 1999
  • 03Behavior-Based Safety (BBS): Managers must positively reinforce safe behaviors, not just punish unsafe ones. IOSH BBS
  • 04Implement a safety management system: Plan → Do → Check → Act (PDCA cycle per BSI PAS 45001). IOSH · BSI
  • 05Analyze near-misses and unsafe conditions with equal priority as accidents — Heinrich's Triangle principle. IOSH Theory
  • 06Safety performance indicators (KPIs) must include both lagging (accident rates) and leading (inspections completed) metrics. IOSH Framework
  • 07Worker competence must be verified before task assignment — training records must be maintained and accessible. IOSH · HSE
  • 08Encourage safety innovation — IOSH supports technology-driven safety solutions including wearables and AI risk tools. IOSH 2024
04
📘 Global Qualification
NEBOSH Safety Principles
🎓
NEBOSH IGC Core Principles
International General Certificate — recognized in 130+ countries
▼
  • 01Risk assessment is the foundation: Identify hazards → Evaluate risks → Implement controls → Monitor effectiveness. NEBOSH IGC
  • 02Apply the Hierarchy of Controls: Eliminate → Substitute → Engineering controls → Admin controls → PPE (last resort). HOC Model
  • 03Incident investigations using the "4 Ps": Person, Place, Plant, Procedures — not just focusing on human error. NEBOSH Method
  • 04Safety responsibilities at every level: Directors → Managers → Supervisors → Workers — all have legal duties. NEBOSH · IOSH
  • 05Monitoring must be proactive (audits, inspections) not reactive (after incidents). Reactive-only systems fail. NEBOSH Unit 1
  • 06Occupational health: Employers must assess and control exposure to noise, dust, chemicals, and ergonomic hazards. NEBOSH OH

🌐 International Standard
ISO 45001 — Occupational Health & Safety
📋
ISO 45001:2018 Requirements
Replaces OHSAS 18001 — world's most used OHS management standard
▼
  • 01Clause 4: Context of the organization — identify internal/external factors affecting OHS objectives. ISO 45001 §4
  • 02Clause 5: Top management must demonstrate leadership by integrating OHS into business strategy, not treating it as a separate function. ISO 45001 §5
  • 03Clause 6: Hazard identification and risk assessment must consider all workers including contractors and visitors. ISO 45001 §6
  • 04Clause 7: Documented information must be controlled — version-controlled procedures, forms, and records. ISO 45001 §7
  • 05Clause 8: Operational planning — emergency preparedness and response procedures must be tested regularly. ISO 45001 §8
  • 06Clause 9: Performance evaluation through internal audits, management review, and KPI monitoring. ISO 45001 §9
  • 07Clause 10: Continual improvement — nonconformities must be corrected and recurrence prevented systematically. ISO 45001 §10
  • 08Worker participation is mandatory — workers must be consulted on hazard identification and control selection. ISO 45001 §5.4

🕊 United Nations Body
ILO — International Labour Standards
🌍
ILO OSH Conventions & Guidelines
Ratified by 180+ countries — the basis of national safety law worldwide
▼
  • 01ILO Convention C155 (1981): Every worker has the fundamental right to a safe and healthy working environment. ILO C155
  • 02ILO Convention C187 (2006): Governments must establish national OSH programs with measurable targets. ILO C187
  • 03ILO P155 Protocol: Work-related injuries must be compensated. Occupational disease must be recorded and reported. ILO P155
  • 04Safety committees must be formed at workplaces of 50+ workers — equal employer and worker representation. ILO C155
  • 05Workers have the right to remove themselves from imminent serious danger without retaliation. ILO C155 Art.13
  • 06ILO estimates 2.78 million work-related deaths occur annually. Most are preventable with proper safety systems. ILO 2023 Data
  • 07Decent Work Agenda: Safety is a human right — not a privilege — and must be accessible regardless of employment status. ILO DWA

🛢 Saudi Arabia
Saudi Aramco HSE Standards
🔶
Aramco SAEP & GI Safety Requirements
Among the world's most rigorous industrial HSE systems
▼
  • 01SAEP-304: All workers must complete Aramco Safety Induction before accessing any Aramco facility. No exceptions. SAEP-304
  • 02GI-6.012: Permit to Work system — 7 types of permits including Hot Work, Confined Space, Electrical, and Excavation. GI-6.012
  • 03GI-0002.102: Drivers on Aramco roads must complete Defensive Driving Course (DDC). Speed governors on all company vehicles. GI-0002.102
  • 04SAEP-327: Scaffolding must be tagged Green (safe), Yellow (restricted), or Red (unsafe). No untagged scaffold is usable. SAEP-327
  • 05SAEP-363: Crane and lifting operations require a Lifting Plan, Rigging Study, and certified rigger/operator. SAEP-363
  • 06GI-1781.001: Life-Saving Rules — 8 non-negotiable rules. Violation results in immediate removal from site. Life-Saving Rules
  • 07SAEP-18: All incidents including near misses, first aid, and unsafe conditions must be reported within 24 hours. SAEP-18
  • 08Zero Harm philosophy: Aramco targets zero fatalities, zero major incidents. HSE KPIs reviewed by CEO quarterly. Aramco Vision

⚙️ Italy / Global
SAIPEM HSSE Management System
🔩
SAIPEM Project HSSE Requirements
Applied on offshore platforms, pipelines, and EPC megaprojects
▼
  • 01SAIPEM requires project-specific HSSE Plans aligned with ISO 45001 and client requirements before project mobilization. SAIPEM HSE
  • 02STOP Cards: Every worker can and must stop any unsafe work using the STOP authority without fear of retaliation. STOP Authority
  • 03Golden Rules: 10 non-negotiable life-saving rules. Breach results in dismissal from project without appeal. Golden Rules
  • 04HAZID/HAZOP studies mandatory during project design phase to identify process hazards before construction begins. HAZID/HAZOP
  • 05Weekly HSSE meetings between project management and site safety teams — minutes documented and tracked. SAIPEM Procedure
  • 06Environmental compliance: SAIPEM applies ISO 14001 alongside OHS — spill prevention and waste management are critical. ISO 14001

Applied Safety
Topic-Based Safety Modules
🔥
Fire Safety Management
NFPA 10, OSHA 1910.157, Aramco GI-0002.000
▼
  • 01Hot work permits required for all welding, cutting, grinding, and open flame operations. Fire watch assigned during and 30 min after. OSHA 1926.352
  • 02Fire extinguishers: Class A (ordinary combustibles), B (flammable liquids), C (electrical), D (metals), K (cooking). Know the right type. NFPA 10
  • 03PASS technique: Pull pin → Aim at base → Squeeze handle → Sweep side to side. Train all workers annually. NFPA
  • 04Emergency exits must be illuminated, unobstructed, and tested monthly. Exit route maps posted at 100m intervals. OSHA 1910.37
  • 05Fire alarm systems tested quarterly. Sprinkler systems inspected annually per NFPA 25. NFPA 25
  • 06Flammable liquids stored in approved containers (FM/UL listed). No more than 25 gallons in workspaces without a safety cabinet. NFPA 30
  • 07Fire drills at minimum twice yearly. Evacuation time recorded and assembly point confirmed every drill. NFPA 101
🪜
Working at Height Safety
OSHA 1926.502, UK WAH Regs 2005, ARAMCO SAEP-327
▼
  • 01Fall protection required at: 4ft (general industry), 6ft (construction), 8ft (scaffolding), 10ft (steel erection). OSHA Standards
  • 02Full-body harness with double lanyard or self-retracting lifeline (SRL) must be 100% tied-off at all times above height limit. OSHA 1926.502
  • 03Scaffolding tagged and inspected before each shift by a competent person. Green tag = safe, Yellow = restricted, Red = do not use. Aramco SAEP-327
  • 04Three-point contact rule for ladder use at all times — two hands and one foot, or two feet and one hand. OSHA Ladder Rules
  • 05Exclusion zones (barricades + safety nets) below aerial work to protect workers from falling objects. UK WAH Regs
  • 06Rescue plan: Before any work at height begins, a rescue plan for fall arrest must be in place and team briefed. IOSH Best Practice
  • 07Suspension Trauma: Workers suspended in a harness after fall must be rescued within 15 minutes to avoid fatal injury. ANSI Z359
🚧
Confined Space Entry
OSHA 1910.146, Aramco GI-6.012.1, ISO 45001 §8
▼
  • 01Atmospheric testing sequence: Oxygen (19.5%–23.5%) → LEL below 10% → CO below 25 ppm → H₂S below 1 ppm. OSHA 1910.146
  • 02Entry permit system: New permit for every entry, every shift. Permit displayed at entry point and inside space. Aramco GI-6.012.1
  • 03Attendant must maintain visual or verbal contact with entrants and track personnel count at all times. OSHA 1910.146
  • 04Emergency retrieval equipment (tripod, lifeline, winch) must be stationed at entry point before work begins. OSHA Standard
  • 05Continuous air monitoring during entry — not just at start. Conditions can change rapidly inside. IOSH Guidance
  • 06Isolation: All energy (electrical, mechanical, pneumatic) and inflows (pipes, valves) must be isolated before entry. LOTO + PTW
⚡
Electrical Safety & LOTO
OSHA 1910.147, NFPA 70E, IEC 60364
▼
  • 01LOTO procedure: Notify → Shut down → Isolate → Lock and tag → Release stored energy → Verify zero energy. OSHA 1910.147
  • 02Arc Flash boundary: NFPA 70E requires arc flash hazard analysis and appropriate PPE (Arc-rated suits, face shields). NFPA 70E
  • 03Only qualified persons (electricians with proof of competency) may work on live circuits above 50V. OSHA 1910.333
  • 04Ground Fault Circuit Interrupters (GFCI) required for all temporary power tools in construction or wet locations. OSHA 1926.404
  • 05Electrical panels must be labeled, cleared of obstructions (36-inch clearance minimum), and accessible for emergency shutoff. NEC 110.26
☣️
Chemical Handling & HazCom
GHS/SDS system, OSHA 1910.1200, REACH (EU)
▼
  • 01GHS labeling: All chemical containers must show product identifier, signal word (Danger/Warning), hazard pictograms, and first aid. GHS · UN
  • 02Safety Data Sheets (SDS) must have 16 sections per GHS format and be accessible to all workers — not locked in office. OSHA 1910.1200
  • 03Secondary containers must be labeled when chemicals are decanted. "Unlabeled" is an automatic violation. OSHA HazCom
  • 04Emergency eyewash stations within 10 seconds travel time from any chemical hazard area — ANSI Z358.1. ANSI Z358.1
  • 05Chemical storage: Incompatible chemicals segregated — acids separated from bases, oxidizers from flammables. NFPA 400
📝
Permit to Work (PTW) System
Aramco GI-6.012, IOSH Guidelines, SAIPEM PTW Procedure
▼
  • 01No work shall commence without an approved, signed permit. Verbal permits do not exist in a safe workplace. Aramco GI-6.012
  • 02PTW types: Cold Work, Hot Work, Confined Space Entry, Electrical Isolation, Excavation, Radiography, Critical Lift. PTW Types
  • 03Permit Issuer (Area Authority) and Permit Receiver (Work Supervisor) must jointly inspect the site before signing. IOSH Framework
  • 04Work must stop and permit re-evaluated if: scope changes, conditions change, shift changes, or incident occurs. SAIPEM PTW
  • 05Permit closure: Site inspection, reinstatement of guards, removal of tools/personnel, and issuer sign-off required. Aramco / SAIPEM
⛓️
Lifting & Rigging Safety
Aramco SAEP-363, ASME B30, BS 7121
▼
  • 01Never exceed the Safe Working Load (SWL/WLL) of slings, hooks, or crane — use the LOWEST rated component. ASME B30
  • 02Critical lifts (>75% crane capacity, over personnel, or unusual conditions) require a written Lift Plan approved by engineer. Aramco SAEP-363
  • 03All lifting equipment inspected: Daily visual check + periodic load test (slings: 6-monthly, cranes: annually). BS 7121
  • 04Exclusion zone: No personnel under suspended load at any time. Tag lines used to control swinging. IOSH / OSHA
  • 05Rigger-to-operator communication: One designated signalman per lift. Pre-agreed hand signals or radio used exclusively. ASME B30.5
🦺
PPE Selection & Management
OSHA 1910.132, EN 365, ANSI Standards
▼
  • 01PPE is the LAST line of defense — controls in the hierarchy (elimination, substitution, engineering) must be exhausted first. HOC Model
  • 02Employer must provide PPE at no cost to workers — OSHA 1910.132(h) amendment. OSHA 1910.132
  • 03Helmet types: Type I (top only) vs Type II (top + lateral). Class E (electrical), G (general), C (conductive). ANSI Z89.1
  • 04Safety footwear: Steel toe (ANSI Z41 or ASTM F2413) required in areas with foot hazards. Metatarsal protection for heavy objects. ASTM F2413
  • 05Hearing protection: Required when noise exceeds 85 dBA (TWA 8hr). Earplugs rated by NRR — select appropriate for exposure level. OSHA 1910.95
  • 06Eye protection: Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1) for basic hazards. Goggles for chemical splash. Face shields for flying particles. ANSI Z87.1
  • 07PPE inspection: Before each use. Harnesses with impact loads must be removed from service immediately. EN 365 · ANSI
🚨
Emergency Response & Preparedness
OSHA 1910.38, ISO 45001 §8.2, NFPA 1600
▼
  • 01Written Emergency Action Plan (EAP) required for all employers with 10+ workers. Must include evacuation routes and roles. OSHA 1910.38
  • 02First aid kits stocked per ANSI Z308.1 — contents based on specific hazards of workplace, not generic kit. ANSI Z308.1
  • 03AED (Automated External Defibrillator) accessible within 3 minutes of any cardiac arrest location — best practice. Heart Assoc.
  • 04Muster points: All evacuation assembly areas posted on site maps and drilled bi-annually. Head count taken by supervisor. ISO 45001 §8.2
  • 05Emergency communication: Single emergency number posted at all phones. Chain of notification documented. NFPA 1600
🏆
Building a Safety Culture
IOSH, NEBOSH, DuPont Bradley Curve, Hearts & Minds
▼
  • 01DuPont Bradley Curve: Safety culture evolves through 4 stages — Reactive → Dependent → Independent → Interdependent. DuPont
  • 02Just Culture: Distinguish between honest mistakes, at-risk behavior, and reckless behavior. Not all incidents are "human error." IOSH Framework
  • 03No-Blame Reporting: Workers must feel psychologically safe to report hazards. Punitive systems reduce reporting by 60%. HSE Research
  • 04Safety leadership walks (management safety tours): Senior leaders must be visible on site, not only in board rooms. IOSH · HSE
  • 05Reward safe behavior — not just zero-accident records. Celebrating near-miss reporting creates stronger cultures. NEBOSH Best Practice
  • 06Hearts & Minds toolkit (Shell): Engage workers emotionally in safety — use stories, examples, not just rules. Shell H&M
🚗
Road & Vehicle Safety
Aramco GI-0002.102, IOSH Road Risk, ISO 39001
▼
  • 01Vehicle Pre-Use Inspection (DRSABC): Dashboard → Rubber/tyres → Safety devices → Accessories → Battery → Checks. SAIPEM / Aramco
  • 02No mobile phone use while driving — even hands-free poses 4x crash risk. Complete ban on many project sites. WHO / Aramco
  • 03Defensive Driving Course (DDC) mandatory before operating any company vehicle — NSC or Aramco-approved course. Aramco GI-0002.102
  • 04Seat belts mandatory for all occupants — all seats, all journeys, regardless of road type or distance. ILO · WHO
  • 05ISO 39001: Road traffic safety management system — allows organizations to systematically reduce road fatalities. ISO 39001
⛏️
Excavation & Trenching Safety
OSHA 1926.652, BS 6164, Aramco SAEP standards
▼
  • 01Any excavation over 1.2m (UK) / 5ft (US) requires a protective system — sloping, shoring, or trench box. OSHA 1926.652
  • 02Underground utility check (CAT/Genny scan) mandatory before any excavation begins. Call 811 (US) / Click Before You Dig. OSHA / Aramco
  • 03Daily inspection by competent person before each shift and after any weather event or vibration nearby. OSHA 1926.651
  • 04Spoil heap minimum 0.6m from trench edge. Heavy equipment must not approach within collapse zone. BS 6164
  • 05Ladders or ramps at maximum 7.5m intervals for quick exit. Workers must not jump into or out of trenches. OSHA 1926.651
⚙️
Mechanical & Machine Safety
OSHA 1910.217, EN ISO 13849, ANSI B11
▼
  • 01Machine guarding: All point-of-operation, power transmission, and nip points must have fixed or interlocked guards. OSHA 1910.212
  • 02Emergency stop (E-stop) buttons: Red, mushroom-shaped, within operator reach and tested at start of each shift. EN ISO 13850
  • 03Safe distance formula: Ds = K × T (safety distance = hand speed constant × stopping time) for presence-sensing devices. EN ISO 13855
  • 04Loose clothing, jewelry, long hair, and gloves (near rotating equipment) must be secured or removed. IOSH / OSHA
  • 05Preventive maintenance schedules must be documented and followed — breakdown maintenance alone is insufficient. ISO 45001
